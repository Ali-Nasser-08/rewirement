### Week 2: Savoring & Gratitude

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Savoring
> Savoring is the act of stepping outside of an experience to review and appreciate it. It forces you to slow down and locks in the memory.
>
> **Techniques:**
> * **Share it:** Tell someone else about the moment.
> * **Memory Building:** Take a mental (or physical) photo.
> * **Self-Congratulation:** Remind yourself how lucky you are.
> * **Sharpen Perception:** Focus on specific sensory details (smell, sound).

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Gratitude
> Gratitude rewires your brain to scan for the positive.
>
> **The Challenge:** Every night, write down **5 things** you are grateful for. They can be huge (promotion) or tiny (good coffee).
> *Key: Don't just list them. Relive the feeling of why you are grateful as you write.*

### Week 3: Kindness & Connection

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Kindness
> Happy people are altruistic. Doing for others boosts your own mood more than doing for yourself.
>
> **The Challenge:** Perform **7 acts of kindness** this week. They can be small: holding a door, buying a coffee for a stranger, sending an encouraging text, or donating to a cause.

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Social Connection
> Social connection is the strongest predictor of happiness. Even brief interactions with strangers matter.
>
> **The Challenge:** Make one new connection per day (chat with a barista, talk to someone on the bus).
> **Deepen:** Once this week, spend a solid hour connecting with someone you care about deeply.

### Week 4: Exercise & Sleep

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Exercise
> Exercise is not just for the body; it's a potent antidepressant.
>
> **The Goal:** Move your body for at least **30 minutes** a day.
> **What counts?** Anything. Walking, yoga, dancing in your room, gym. Just get your heart rate up and notice the mood boost afterwards.

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Sleep Hygiene
> Sleep deprivation kills mood and cognitive function.
>
> **The Goal:** Get at least **7 hours** of sleep for 4 nights this week.
> **How:** No screens 30 mins before bed; No caffeine after 2 PM; Make your room dark and cool.

### Week 5: Meditation & Letter

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Meditation
> Meditation builds the muscle of attention, allowing you to get off the "hedonic treadmill" of constant wanting.
>
> **The Goal:** Meditate for **10 minutes** every day.
> **How:** Focus on your breath. When your mind wanders (and it will), gently bring it back. That "bringing back" is the rep.

> [!TIP] Learning Concept: Gratitude Letter
> This is a "nuclear" happiness boost. Write a letter (300+ words) to someone who changed your life but whom you never properly thanked.
>
> **The Visit:** Ideally, meet them in person or call them, and read the letter *out loud* to them. It creates a powerful shared moment.
